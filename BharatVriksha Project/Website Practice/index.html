About Us

The Konark Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Odisha, India, is an architectural marvel dedicated to the Sun God, Surya. Built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, the temple is designed in the shape of a colossal chariot with intricately carved stone wheels, horses, and pillars, symbolizing the Sun God’s journey across the sky. Renowned for its exquisite carvings depicting mythological scenes, celestial beings, and everyday life, the temple stands as a testament to India’s rich cultural and artistic heritage. Despite partial ruin over the centuries, Konark remains a significant historical and spiritual landmark, attracting visitors from around the world.

Speciality

Chariot-Shaped Architecture

The temple is designed as a massive chariot of Surya (Sun God), with 24 intricately carved stone wheels and seven horses

Intricate Stone Carvings

The walls are adorned with detailed sculptures depicting deities, celestial beings, warriors, dancers, and daily life, showcasing the finest craftsmanship of Kalinga architecture.

Magnetic and Engineering Marvels

The temple was believed to have a magnetically suspended idol at its sanctum, and its architectural alignment allows the first rays of the rising sun to pass through the temple entrance, illuminating the deity.
