About Us

The Lotus Temple, also known as the Baháʼí House of Worship, is an architectural marvel located in New Delhi, India. Designed to resemble a blooming lotus flower, the temple is a symbol of peace, unity, and the oneness of humanity. The temple is constructed from white marble, with a glass-and-steel roof that allows natural light to illuminate the interior Its unique structure consists of 27 free-standing marble petals arranged in clusters, forming nine sides that represent the Baháʼí principle of inclusivity, welcoming people of all faiths.
